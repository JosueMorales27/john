Why do you need an Unshorten URL tool?
An unshorten URL tool is used to expand or resolve shortened URLs into their original long form. Here are some reasons why you might need an unshorten URL tool:

Security: Shortened URLs are often used in phishing scams or to hide malicious links. Unshortening a URL can help you identify if the link is legitimate or if it leads to a malicious site.
Accuracy: Shortened URLs can sometimes be confusing or difficult to understand, especially if they are not labeled with clear context or information. Unshortening a URL can help you better understand where the link is leading to, and can help you avoid clicking on links that you are not interested in.
Research: If you are conducting research or investigating a topic, unshortening URLs can help you identify the original source of a link, which can be useful for citation or attribution purposes.
Analytics: If you are a website owner or marketer, unshortening URLs can help you track clicks and traffic coming from shortened links. By unshortening URLs, you can get a better understanding of where your traffic is coming from and how users are interacting with your content.
CONCLUSION
An unshorten URL tool is a useful tool for anyone who wants to expand or resolve shortened URLs into their original long form. It can help you avoid security risks, improve accuracy and understanding, aid in research and attribution, and provide insights into website traffic and performance.
